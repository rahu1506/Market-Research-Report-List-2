<p><p>A capillary rheometer is an instrument used to measure the rheological properties of fluids, specifically their flow behavior. It works based on the principle that when a fluid is subjected to a constant pressure, the flow rate is proportional to the applied pressure and inversely proportional to the fluid's viscosity. Capillary rheometers are widely used in various industries, including plastics, rubber, chemicals, and pharmaceuticals, to determine the processing and flow properties of materials.</p><p>The capillary rheometer market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for advanced materials with precise flow properties and the growing need for quality control in various industries are driving the market growth. Moreover, the growing adoption of capillary rheometers in the research and development activities of new materials is also contributing to the market expansion.</p><p>In terms of trends, technological advancements in capillary rheometer instruments are gaining traction.
