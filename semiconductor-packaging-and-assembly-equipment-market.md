Die-level packaging and assembly equipment is responsible for processing individual chips or dies, which are then packaged into a single unit. On the other hand, wafer-level packaging and assembly equipment handles the packaging of entire wafers with multiple chips. While die-level packaging focuses on individual components, wafer-level packaging streamlines the process by packaging multiple chips simultaneously, saving time and cost in semiconductor production.</p></p>
