<p><p>Tip-enhanced Raman Spectroscopy (TERS) equipment is an advanced analytical technique used for surface analysis with high spatial resolution. TERS combines Raman spectroscopy with a scanning probe microscope (SPM) to achieve nanoscale resolution. This technique utilizes a sharp metallic probe, often coated with a thin layer of silver or gold, which is brought in close proximity to the sample surface. The interaction between the probe and the sample enhances the Raman signal, enabling the detection of very small concentrations of molecules.</p><p>The TERS equipment market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.4% during the forecast period. One of the major drivers for market growth is the increasing demand for nanoscale surface analysis in various industries such as semiconductor, pharmaceutical, and materials science. TERS offers a unique capability to study the chemical composition and structure of materials at the nanoscale, which is crucial for research and development activities.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in technology have led to the development of more efficient and user-friendly TERS instruments, which is further driving the market growth. The integration of TERS with other microscopy techniques, such as atomic force microscopy (AFM) and scanning electron microscopy (SEM), has also expanded its application areas.</p><p>In terms of trends, there is a growing focus on the development of portable and handheld TERS equipment, which would enable on-field analysis and real-time monitoring. Additionally, there is a rising demand for TERS equipment with better resolution and sensitivity to detect even smaller concentrations of molecules.</p><p>In conclusion, the TERS equipment market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing need for nanoscale surface analysis and technological advancements. The market is expected to continue growing at a steady pace in the coming years.</p></p>

On the other hand, the "Other" market category covers alternative technologies or devices used for Raman spectroscopy applications, such as surface-enhanced Raman spectroscopy (SERS) or confocal Raman microscopy.
