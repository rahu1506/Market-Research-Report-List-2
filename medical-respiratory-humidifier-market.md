<p><p>A medical respiratory humidifier is a device used to add moisture to the air delivered to a patient's respiratory system. It is often used in hospitals and other healthcare settings to prevent drying of the airways in patients receiving respiratory support, such as those on mechanical ventilation or with tracheostomy tubes. The medical respiratory humidifier helps in maintaining the humidity levels of the inhaled air, preventing complications like dryness, irritation, and mucus plugs in the respiratory system.</p><p>The medical respiratory humidifier market is expected to witness substantial growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases, such as asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and pneumonia, is one of the primary factors driving market growth. <p><p>The Medical Respiratory Humidifier Market offers two main types of humidifiers: Active Type and Passive Type. Active Type humidifiers use electrical energy to generate heat and energy, which then transfers moisture to the air. They provide precise humidity control and can efficiently deliver moisture over a long period of time. On the other hand, Passive Type humidifiers do not require electricity and work by natural evaporation or diffusion. They are cost-effective and easy to operate but may have limitations in terms of humidity control.</p></p>
