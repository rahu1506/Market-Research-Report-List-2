<p><p>Multi-parameter patient monitoring devices are categorized based on the level of acuity of patients they are designed for. High-acuity monitors are used in intensive care units and operating rooms for patients requiring constant monitoring of vital signs. Mid-acuity monitors are used in step-down units and general wards for patients needing periodic monitoring. Low-acuity monitors are used in outpatient settings or home care for patients requiring occasional monitoring. Each type of monitor is tailored to meet the specific needs of patients based on their level of acuity.</p></p>
