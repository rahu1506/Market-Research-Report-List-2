<p><p>Scanning Tunneling Microscopes (STMs) are used to observe and analyze objects at the atomic level. There are two main types of STMs: Constant-Current Mode and Constant-Height Mode. In Constant-Current Mode, the current flowing between the STM tip and the sample is kept constant, while the distance between the tip and the sample varies. This mode provides high-resolution images of the sample's surface topography. In Constant-Height Mode, the distance between the tip and the sample is kept constant, while the current varies. This mode allows for studying the electronic properties and conductivity of the sample.</p></p>
