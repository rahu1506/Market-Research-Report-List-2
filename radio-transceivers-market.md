<p><p>Radio transceivers can be categorized into two types: programmable and not programmable. Programmable transceivers allow users to modify and update settings, such as frequency, modulation, and power, according to their specific needs. On the other hand, non-programmable transceivers come with fixed settings that cannot be changed by the user. Programmable transceivers offer flexibility and versatility, allowing users to adapt their devices to different applications, while non-programmable transceivers are simpler and more straightforward but lack customization options. The choice between these types depends on the user's requirements and preferences.</p></p>
