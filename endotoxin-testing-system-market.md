<p><ul><li>Turbidimetric</li><li>Chromogenic</li><li>rFC Assays</li><li>Gel Clot</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Endotoxin Testing System market offers various types of testing methods to detect the presence of endotoxins, harmful substances produced by certain bacteria. Turbidimetric methods measure changes in light transmission caused by the interaction between endotoxins and reagents. Chromogenic methods use a color change reaction to indicate the presence of endotoxins. rFC assays employ recombinant factor C protein as a substitute for horseshoe crab-derived factor C. Gel clot tests involve the formation of a gel clot when endotoxins are present. Lastly, there are other methods available in the market that also offer accurate endotoxin detection.</p></p>
