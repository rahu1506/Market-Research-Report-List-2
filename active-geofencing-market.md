<p><p>Active geofencing is a location-based technology that uses GPS, cellular data, or Wi-Fi signals to create a virtual perimeter around a specific geographic area. It enables businesses to send targeted and personalized messages, notifications, or advertisements to users' mobile devices when they enter or exit the designated area. Unlike passive geofencing, active geofencing actively engages with users by delivering real-time content based on their location.</p><p>The active geofencing market is witnessing significant growth due to increasing demand for location-based marketing and personalized advertising strategies. The rising adoption of smartphones and the growing popularity of mobile apps have amplified the potential of active geofencing. Businesses across various sectors, including retail, transportation, hospitality, and healthcare, are utilizing this technology to enhance customer engagement, drive footfall, and boost sales.</p><p>Furthermore, the active geofencing market is driven by advancements in location-based services, improvement in data accuracy and precision, and the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms. These advancements enable businesses to deliver more relevant and timely messages to users, improving the effectiveness of their marketing campaigns.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the active geofencing market is the integration of beacon technology. Beacons are small wireless devices that can be placed in specific locations to transmit signals to nearby smartphones. The combination of active geofencing and beacon technology allows businesses to deliver hyper-targeted messages and promotions to users when they are in close proximity to a particular point of interest.</p><p>Overall, the active geofencing market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 13.8% during the forecast period. <p><p>Active geofencing is a location-based marketing technology that uses GPS or RFID to trigger actions when a user enters or exits a specified geographical area. Fixed market geofencing applies to a predetermined set location, like a retail store, and sends targeted messages to nearby smartphones. Mobile market geofencing, on the other hand, follows users as they move and sends personalized ads or notifications based on their current location. Both types aim to engage customers by providing relevant information or offers based on their proximity to specific locations.</p></p>
