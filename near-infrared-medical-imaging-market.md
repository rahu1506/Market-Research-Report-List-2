<p><ul><li>Near Infrared Fluorescence Imaging Devices</li><li>Near Infrared Bioluminescence Imaging Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Near infrared medical imaging refers to the use of near infrared light to visualize and study biological processes in the body. Near infrared fluorescence imaging devices employ fluorescent dyes to target and detect specific molecules or cells in tissues. On the other hand, near infrared bioluminescence imaging devices use bioluminescent proteins or luciferase enzymes to emit light signals, enabling visualization of biological activities. Both imaging techniques offer non-invasive and real-time monitoring of various physiological and pathological processes, thus providing valuable insights for biomedical research and clinical applications.</p></p>
