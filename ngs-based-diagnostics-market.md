<p><p>NGS (Next Generation Sequencing) based diagnostics refers to the use of advanced genomic technologies and methods to analyze and interpret genetic information for diagnostic purposes. It has become a powerful tool in precision medicine, personalized healthcare, and oncology diagnostics.</p><p>The NGS based diagnostics market is witnessing significant growth due to several factors. Firstly, the declining cost of sequencing is making NGS technology more accessible to healthcare providers and researchers. This has boosted the use of NGS technology in clinical settings, leading to better patient outcomes and personalized treatment options.</p><p>Another key driver of market growth is the increasing incidence of chronic diseases and genetic disorders, which requires accurate and comprehensive genomic profiling for effective diagnosis and treatment. <p><p>NGS based diagnostics is a rapidly evolving market that encompasses several types of sequencing technologies. DNA sequencing involves determining the precise order of nucleotides in a DNA molecule. RNA sequencing focuses on analyzing the transcriptome, or the set of all RNA molecules present in a cell or tissue at a given time. Whole genome sequencing aims to sequence the entire genome of an organism. On the other hand, whole exome sequencing targets the protein-coding regions of the genome, which are responsible for most genetic diseases. Lastly, other markets include applications like metagenomics or targeted sequencing for specific genes or regions of interest.</p></p>
