<p><p>The Respiratory Electrical Stimulation Device (RESD) market is divided into two types: external and internal. The external type refers to devices that are placed outside the body and connected to the respiratory muscles through electrodes or sensors, providing electrical stimulation to these muscles. On the other hand, the internal type involves devices implanted inside the body, directly stimulating the respiratory muscles. Both types aim to assist individuals with respiratory difficulties, such as spinal cord injuries or neuromuscular diseases, to improve their breathing capacity and overall respiratory function.</p></p>
