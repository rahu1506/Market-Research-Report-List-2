<p><p>The 3D printing in jewellery market consists of several types, including Stereolithography (SLA), Selective Laser Sintering (SLS), Digital Light Processing (DLP), Fused Depositing Modelling (FDM), and others. SLA utilizes a laser to solidify liquid resin layer by layer, creating precise and detailed jewellery designs. SLS employs a laser to fuse powdered materials together, offering greater design flexibility. DLP employs a digital light projector to cure liquid resin, enabling faster production. FDM involves heating and extruding filaments of materials to create jewellery. Other methods in this market include electronic beam melting and laminated object manufacturing.</p></p>
