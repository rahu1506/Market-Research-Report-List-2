<p><p>Photovoltaic systems are categorized into different types based on the materials used in solar cell manufacturing. Single crystal silicon, also known as monocrystalline silicon, refers to solar cells made from a single crystal structure, offering high efficiency but at a higher cost. Polycrystalline silicon, or multicrystalline silicon, comprises solar cells made from multiple crystal structures, providing a balance between efficiency and affordability. The "Others" category encompasses alternative materials like thin-film solar cells, such as amorphous silicon, cadmium telluride, and copper indium gallium selenide, offering lower efficiency but lighter weight and potential cost benefits.</p></p>
