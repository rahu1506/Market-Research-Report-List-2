<p><p>Water softeners are devices used to remove minerals such as calcium and magnesium from hard water in order to improve its quality. There are two main types of water softeners on the market: salt-based water softeners and salt-free water softeners. Salt-based water softeners use a process called ion exchange to replace the minerals with sodium ions, while salt-free water softeners use a technique called template-assisted crystallization to alter the structure of the minerals, preventing them from adhering to surfaces. Both types offer different solutions for addressing the issue of hard water and can be selected based on individual needs and preferences.</p></p>
