<p><p>Distributed amplifiers are divided into two main types: wideband distributed amplifiers and broadband distributed amplifiers. Wideband distributed amplifiers are designed to amplify signals across a wide frequency range, usually from a few megahertz to several gigahertz. On the other hand, broadband distributed amplifiers are specifically focused on amplifying signals over a wide frequency band, typically covering the entire frequency range of interest. Both types of amplifiers are commonly used in various applications, such as telecommunications, RF & microwave systems, and high-speed data transmission.</p></p>
