<p><ul><li>Taylor Discrete Model</li><li>Taylor Continuous Model</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Taylor Dispersion Analysis is a technique used for characterizing flow in chromatography and microfluidic devices. The technique includes two types of market models: Taylor Discrete Model and Taylor Continuous Model. The Taylor Discrete Model is suitable for studying flow patterns in open capillaries and porous media, as it considers discrete paths. On the other hand, the Taylor Continuous Model is utilized for analyzing flow in narrow capillaries, focusing on continuous pathways. Both models have their specific applications in understanding flow behavior and optimizing the design of flow systems.</p></p>
