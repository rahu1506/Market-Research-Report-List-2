<p><p>Tourniquets are devices used to apply pressure on a limb to control bleeding. There are three main types of tourniquets in the market: buckle, manual, and electronic. Buckle tourniquets are the most common and feature a buckle mechanism to tighten and secure the device. Manual tourniquets require manual twisting or cranking to apply pressure. Electronic tourniquets are the most advanced, utilizing electronic controls to regulate pressure automatically. These tourniquet types offer varying degrees of ease of use and control, catering to different preferences and medical requirements.</p></p>
