<p><p>Peripheral Intravenous (IV) Catheters are divided into several types based on their design and functionality. Short PIVCs are typically used for short-term infusion therapy and have a shorter length compared to standard IV catheters. Integrated or Closed PIVCs combine a catheter and needle into a single unit, reducing the risk of needle-stick injuries and contamination. These types of PIVCs are often used in high-risk patients or in settings where infection control is critical, such as intensive care units.</p></p>
