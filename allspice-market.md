<p><p>The Allspice market offers three main types: Ground Allspice, Whole Allspice, and Rub Allspice. Ground Allspice refers to the powdered form of the dried berries of the allspice plant, commonly used in cooking and baking. Whole Allspice consists of the dried berries in their intact form, used for infusing flavor in soups, sauces, and pickling. Rub Allspice involves a mixture of ground allspice with other spices, herbs, and seasonings, typically used as a dry rub for marinades and seasoning meats. All three variations provide unique ways to incorporate the rich and aromatic flavor of allspice into various culinary creations.</p></p>
