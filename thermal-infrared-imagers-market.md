<p><p>Thermal infrared imagers, also known as thermal cameras or thermal imagers, are devices that use infrared radiation to create images of objects or scenes. Thermal infrared imagers find applications in various sectors, including military and defense, industrial, automotive, healthcare, and surveillance and security.</p><p>The market for thermal infrared imagers has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 4.60% during the forecast period. This integration enables advanced features such as temperature anomaly detection, automatic target tracking, and predictive maintenance, enhancing the overall performance and efficiency of thermal imaging systems. Another emerging trend is the miniaturization of thermal cameras, making them more compact and portable for use in handheld devices, smartphones, and wearables.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing adoption of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) or drones equipped with thermal infrared imagers is also contributing to the market growth. These UAVs provide real-time thermal imaging for various applications, including search and rescue operations, environmental monitoring, and infrastructure inspection.</p><p>In conclusion, the thermal infrared imagers market is witnessing steady growth, driven by factors such as increasing adoption in surveillance and security, advancements in sensor technology, and integration of AI and ML algorithms.
