<p><p>Passive dosimeters are devices used to measure and record the cumulative dose of radiation exposure over a period of time. Optically Stimulated Luminescence (OSL) dosimeters use crystals that emit light when exposed to radiation. These dosimeters are highly sensitive and provide accurate and reliable measurements. Thermoluminescent dosimeters (TLD) also use crystals, but they emit light when heated. These dosimeters are widely used in medical and industrial applications. Additionally, there are other types of passive dosimeters available in the market, each with their own unique features and benefits for monitoring radiation exposure.</p></p>
