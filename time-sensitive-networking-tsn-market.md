<p><p>Time-Sensitive Networking (TSN) refers to a set of standards governed by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) that enable real-time communication in Ethernet networks. TSN focuses on various market types, including IEEE 802.1 AS (Audio Video Bridging), IEEE 802.1 Qca (Path Control and Reservation), IEEE 802.1 AS-Rev (Time Synchronization), IEEE 802.1 Qbv (Enhancements for Scheduled Traffic), IEEE 802.1 Qci (Stream Reservation Protocol), IEEE 802.1 CB (Frame Replication and Elimination for Reliability), IEEE 802.1 Qcc (Stream Reservation Protocol for Frame-Based Traffic), IEEE 802.1 Qch (Frame Preemption), and IEEE 802.1 CM (Configuration and Management). These standards ensure precise timing, improved network performance, and quality of service in various industry applications.</p></p>
