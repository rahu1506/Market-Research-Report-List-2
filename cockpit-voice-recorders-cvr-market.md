<p><p>Cockpit Voice Recorders (CVR) are commonly used in aircraft to record audio communications and sounds in the cockpit during flights. Embedded Type CVRs are integrated into the aircraft's avionics system and are smaller and lightweight. They offer higher levels of reliability and storage capacity. On the other hand, Ordinary Type CVRs are standalone devices that can be easily removed and inspected. They have a limited storage capacity and are typically used in smaller or older aircraft where the integration of embedded systems is not feasible or cost-effective.</p></p>
