<p><p>Mobile payment, also known as mobile money, refers to a method of monetary transactions conducted through a mobile device. The market types in mobile payment include remote and proximity markets. In the remote market, users can make payments from anywhere using their mobile devices, eliminating the need for physical interaction. In contrast, proximity market transactions involve users making payments in-person at a physical point-of-sale terminal equipped with near-field communication technology, where the mobile device is held near the terminal to initiate the payment. Both remote and proximity markets offer convenient and secure ways for users to make monetary transactions using their mobile devices.</p></p>
