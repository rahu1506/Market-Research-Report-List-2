<p><p>Acoustic emission sensors are used to detect and monitor structural integrity by capturing high-frequency sound waves emitted by materials under stress. The resonance model of these sensors is highly sensitive in a specific frequency range, making it suitable for detecting defects in specific materials. Wide bandwidth models, on the other hand, have a broader frequency range and are typically used in non-destructive testing. Special models are designed for specific applications, such as underwater monitoring or monitoring of high-temperature environments. Each type offers unique capabilities to cater to various industry requirements.</p></p>
